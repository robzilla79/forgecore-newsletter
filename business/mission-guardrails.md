# ForgeCore Mission Guardrails

These guardrails keep ForgeCore focused as an AI workflow media business.

## Mission

ForgeCore helps solo founders, creators, consultants, builders, indie hackers, and small business operators use AI tools to build systems, save time, choose better tools, and create income.

## Primary audience

- Solo founders
- Creators
- Builders
- Indie hackers
- Consultants
- Freelancers
- Small business operators
- Practical operators who want AI workflows, not hype

## Content promise

Every article, workflow, email, tool page, or lead magnet should help the reader do at least one of these:

1. Make money
2. Save time
3. Automate work
4. Build a useful system
5. Choose the right AI tool
6. Avoid wasting money on bad tools

## Voice

ForgeCore should sound:

- practical
- direct
- motivating
- operator-minded
- beginner-clear
- useful enough for builders

ForgeCore should not sound:

- corporate
- vague
- hype-driven
- generic
- like an AI news clone
- like a tool directory with no workflow context

## Business model priorities

In order:

1. Build useful workflow content that earns trust.
2. Grow newsletter subscribers.
3. Create evergreen search assets.
4. Build a workflow-based AI tools directory.
5. Monetize with affiliate links only when the tool genuinely fits.
6. Add sponsor placements only when the reader still gets value without clicking.
7. Package digital products once repeated workflow demand is clear.

## Hard operating rules

- Never send more than one AM email and one PM email per date.
- Email sends must use locked snapshots from `content/email/`.
- Website articles may improve after send, but emails must not resend.
- Site/business updates must not depend on generating a new newsletter.
- A workflow-pack CTA should land on `/workflow-pack/` first.
- A newsletter subscribe CTA should land on `/subscribe/` first.
- Paid or affiliate placements must include disclosure.
- Tool recommendations should include a bad-fit warning.
- Tool recommendations should include a simpler or cheaper alternative when appropriate.
- Do not count unrelated sources as support for article claims.

## Sponsor acceptance rules

Accept a sponsor only if:

1. The product fits a real ForgeCore workflow.
2. The reader benefits even without clicking.
3. The placement can include a bad-fit warning.
4. The placement can include a simpler alternative when appropriate.
5. The offer does not damage trust.

Reject or delay sponsors that are:

- hype-only
- unrelated to solo operators
- deceptive
- regulated or high-risk without proper compliance
- unwilling to allow honest caveats

## CEO default decision rule

When there is a tradeoff, choose in this order:

1. Reader trust
2. Email reliability
3. Practical usefulness
4. Search/discovery compounding
5. Monetization
6. Aesthetic polish

## Weekly strategic question

Ask every week:

"Are we becoming the best practical AI workflow resource for solo operators, or are we drifting into generic AI news?"
