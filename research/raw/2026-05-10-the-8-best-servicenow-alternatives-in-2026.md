I love my air fryer as much as the next millennial, but it's too easy to treat it like a magical appliance that can handle everything. While you can roast vegetables, reheat leftovers, make crispy chickpeas, and cook salmon in 12 minutes flat, you're not searing a steak in that thing. And if you're making fries for a crowd, you'll spend the next hour baking painfully small batches while everyone waits.
ServiceNow is the enterprise software equivalent. It covers enterprise ITSM, AI workflow automation, project portfolio management, customer service, and more. It's the industry standard for a reason. But it also comes with a six-figure price tag, a months-long implementation cycle, and a gargantuan feature set most organizations will never fully use. Sometimes you need to ditch the fancy air fryer for a purpose-built tool that'll do the job better, cheaper, and faster.
These are your best ServiceNow alternatives for each piece of that puzzle, so you can find exactly what you need without buying the whole package.

A good ServiceNow alternative doesn't need to do everything ServiceNow does—it needs to do the specific thing you're actually picking it for, and do it well. That said, I evaluated every pick against a consistent set of criteria:
Fit for purpose. ServiceNow is an ITSM platform that's expanded into AI workflow automation, PPM, customer service, and more. The best alternatives are purpose-built for the slice of this massive pie they actually serve.
Implementation fit. ServiceNow's months-long implementation cycles are necessary in some cases, but some teams need quicker time-to-value, so I kept that in mind.
Integration and automation depth. Automation is at the heart of ServiceNow, so all these alternatives need to build on that, either natively or with Zapier.
Value. ServiceNow's pricing is opaque, to say the least. I mostly looked for alternatives that offer clearer pricing, since that massive line item is a common reason folks are looking for ServiceNow alternatives to begin with.
Scalability. The best alternatives grow with you. A 10-person team probably doesn't need ServiceNow, but as they grow, they'll need something that can grow with them.
Based on thousands of hours of testing by the Zapier team, as well as my own experiences evaluating and testing this kind of software, these are my picks for the best ServiceNow alternatives.

Zapier lets you build end-to-end IT automation workflows that eliminate manual handoffs entirely. It connects every tool in your organization and automates what happens between them, without a single line of code.
For example, when a new incident is created in your monitoring platform, Zapier can simultaneously open a ticket in your service desk, post to a Slack channel with priority routing logic, create a Jira issue for the engineering team, and assign it to the right agent based on predefined rules—all automatically. No one writes that workflow in ServiceNow in an afternoon. You can also add AI at any step in the process and orchestrate the entire workflow autonomously. And you can access Zapier's vast integration library directly from your favorite AI tools with Zapier MCP.
Zapier Copilot lets you describe what you want in plain language and get a working automation draft, which means your marketing, sales, and HR teams can automate their own workflows without filing a request with IT. But for enterprises, Zapier still gives IT the security, visibility, and control they need, with SOC 2 Type II, GDPR, and CCPA compliance, SSO and SCIM provisioning, audit logs, and role-based access controls.
Zapier isn't going to replace ServiceNow's CMDB or change management governance, but if what you need is a no-code automation and AI orchestration layer that connects your whole stack, Zapier is hard to beat.
