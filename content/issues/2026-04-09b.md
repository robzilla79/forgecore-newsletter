# FORGE/DAILY — April 9, 2026 (Evening)
*AI news for people who don't need it explained twice.*

---

## THE STORY

Anthropic announced Claude Mythos today and immediately declined to release it. Not a soft launch, not a staged rollout — a hard gate. Fifty companies get preview access. Everyone else gets a press release and $25/M input token pricing to think about.

The stated reason is cybersecurity risk. Anthropic says Mythos is capable enough in offensive security domains that broad access creates genuine danger. Whether you believe that framing or not, the decision itself is notable: a frontier lab voluntarily withholding its best model isn't a PR stunt, it's a structural shift in how these companies think about deployment risk.

What's interesting is the timing. This lands one week after Google shipped Gemma 4 open-weight, and the same week Zhipu dropped GLM-5.1 at 744B parameters under MIT license for free. The open-weight ecosystem is accelerating. Anthropic's response is to go in the opposite direction — more controlled, more expensive, more gated.

The split is no longer about capability. At the frontier, several models are roughly equivalent. It's about control: who gets access, on what terms, and who decides. Mythos is Anthropic planting a flag on one side of that argument.

Anthropic saying "we built something too dangerous to ship" is either the most responsible thing a lab has ever done or the best marketing copy in the industry — and I genuinely can't tell which. The fifty companies getting preview access aren't charities. Watch what they build with it.
