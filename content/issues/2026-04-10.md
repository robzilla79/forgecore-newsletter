## THE STORY

**Anthropic built something scary enough to make Powell and Bessent call an emergency meeting.**

On April 8, Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and Fed Chair Jerome Powell did something that doesn't happen: they summoned the CEOs of the biggest banks in America to Treasury headquarters to talk about an AI model. Not regulation in the abstract. Not "AI risk" as a category. A specific model. Anthropic's **Claude Mythos** — which Anthropic itself says can identify and exploit vulnerabilities in every major operating system and web browser when directed by a user to do so.

That last clause is doing a lot of work. "When directed by a user" is the part that keeps security people up at night. Mythos isn't some rogue system — it's a tool. A very capable one. Capable enough that Anthropic is releasing it only to a small set of carefully-vetted parties, and capable enough that the US Treasury decided the financial system needed a heads-up.

This is not hype. When Powell leaves the Fed building to warn bankers about a software product, something real is happening. Whether Mythos lives up to the billing or not, the precedent is set: frontier AI is now a systemic financial risk conversation, not just a tech one.

## EM'S TAKE

The Mythos meeting is the most interesting thing to happen in AI this week and possibly this month. Not because the model is necessarily as dangerous as advertised — Anthropic has obvious incentives to make their product sound terrifyingly powerful — but because of what the meeting *means*. The US government now treats a specific AI release as a systemic risk event. That's a line we just crossed. File it under: things that seemed theoretical until they weren't.
