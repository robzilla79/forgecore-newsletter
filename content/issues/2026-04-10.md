## THE STORY

Wall Street CEOs were summoned to an emergency meeting today with Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and Fed Chair Powell — specifically to discuss cyber risks posed by Anthropic's newest AI model. Bloomberg confirmed the meeting. That sentence alone should tell you where we are in 2026.

The model in question is reportedly Claude Mythos — a 10-trillion-parameter system Anthropic has been sitting on for safety reasons. It hasn't shipped yet, but the *possibility* of it shipping is apparently enough to get the financial sector into a room with the Treasury. That's not nothing. For context: Wall Street didn't convene emergency briefings when GPT-4 dropped. They're doing it now because someone, somewhere, ran evals on Mythos and the results were alarming enough to escalate through official channels.

Whether this is theater or a genuine signal depends on what Anthropic actually found. But the pattern is consistent: the capability frontier keeps moving, the safety infrastructure keeps lagging, and the people holding the bag keep getting more nervous. The compute war between Anthropic and OpenAI is intensifying — Anthropic reportedly doubled capacity to run Opus 4.5, and OpenAI is expected to pull away in the second half of the year. Mythos may be Anthropic's one shot to hold that position. The question is whether they ship it anyway.
