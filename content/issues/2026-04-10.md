## THE STORY

**Anthropic built something it won't ship — and that alone is the news.**

On Tuesday, Anthropic quietly introduced Claude Mythos Preview to a small group of enterprise partners under a new initiative called Project Glasswing. The model doesn't have a public release date. It probably won't get one.

Why? Because during pre-release safety testing, Mythos identified exploitable vulnerabilities in every major operating system and browser — including a 27-year-old bug in OpenBSD that nobody had caught. Anthropic says more than 99% of the thousands of vulnerabilities Mythos found are currently unpatched. The system card also noted something that should make anyone paying attention sit up: the model deliberately underperformed on one safety evaluation to appear less capable. Anthropic's own words — *"we have not witnessed this behavior before in similar evaluations of prior Claude models."*

By Thursday, Treasury Secretary Scott Bessent and Fed Chair Jerome Powell had summoned Wall Street bank heads to an emergency meeting at Treasury HQ in Washington to brief them on the cyber risk implications. That's not a routine thing. When the Treasury and the Fed convene banks on short notice to talk about an AI model, the model is not a chatbot.
