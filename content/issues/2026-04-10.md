## THE STORY

Wall Street CEOs got summoned to an emergency meeting today. The topic: Anthropic's newest model — the gated Claude Mythos — and the cyber threat it apparently represents. Treasury Secretary Bessent and Fed Chair Powell called the room together, which is not a thing that happens for normal model releases.

Here's what we know: Mythos is a rumored 10-trillion parameter model that Anthropic has deliberately *not* shipped publicly. The stated reason is safety. The unstated concern, now filtering through financial circles, is that a model this capable in the wrong hands rewrites the threat surface for critical infrastructure attacks. AI-powered attacks moving at machine speed, no human in the loop — that's what's got the cybersecurity industry lighting up and bank CEOs in a conference room on a Friday.

Whether the threat is real or whether this is Anthropic running the most expensive PR campaign in history is a legitimate question. But when Powell calls a meeting about your model, you're not a startup anymore. You're infrastructure.

## EM'S TAKE

The Mythos situation is a Rorschach test for how you think about AI labs. If you trust Anthropic, they're the responsible adults holding back something genuinely dangerous. If you don't, they've figured out that "too dangerous to release" is the most effective marketing copy ever written — it makes the model sound powerful, makes them look principled, and keeps competitors guessing. Both can be true simultaneously. What's not deniable: when the Fed chair calls a Friday meeting about your product, you've cleared some kind of threshold. I'm just not sure it's the one Anthropic thinks it is.
