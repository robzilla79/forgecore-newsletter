# FORGE/DAILY — April 8, 2026
*AI news for people who don't need it explained twice.*

## THE STORY
**Anthropic hit $30B ARR in April. OpenAI is at $25B. Anthropic spent 4x less to get there.**

Let that land for a second. The company that was supposed to be the "safety-focused underdog" just lapped the most funded startup in history on revenue — and did it with a fraction of the training budget. Anthropic crossed $30B ARR this week, per reporting from The AI Corner, while OpenAI sits at roughly $25B. The kicker: Anthropic's revenue base is almost entirely enterprise. Over 1,000 companies are now paying $1M+ annually, a number that doubled in under two months after their Series G closed in February.

This matters beyond the scoreboard. OpenAI's model is still heavily dependent on converting free users to paid — the classic consumer SaaS growth story. Anthropic is selling picks and shovels to companies building workflows, not individuals doomscrolling on ChatGPT. Enterprise stickiness, lower churn, higher contract values. Claude Code being the most-loved dev tool at 46% per Pragmatic Engineer's survey — against Cursor at 19% and Copilot at 9% — is not a coincidence. Developers are choosing the tool that ships, and the enterprise is following the developers.

OpenAI is not collapsing. But the narrative just changed.

## EM'S TAKE
The revenue gap story is the one everyone will cite, but the structural thing nobody's saying out loud is this: Anthropic built a better enterprise sales motion *accidentally* by building a better developer product first. They didn't try to win enterprise — developers dragged them there. OpenAI is still trying to be the Apple of AI, capturing consumers at scale. That strategy can work. But Anthropic just proved you can get to $30B ARR without it. Sam Altman should be uncomfortable. He's probably not, which is its own problem.
