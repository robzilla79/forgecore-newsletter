## THE STORY
**Meta shipped Muse Spark today. It's fine. That's not the interesting part.**

After nine months and a $14B bet on Alexandr Wang and Meta Superintelligence Labs, Meta's first post-Llama flagship model is... competitive. Muse Spark lands roughly on par with leading models from OpenAI, Google, and Anthropic on writing and reasoning. It underperforms on coding, which Meta acknowledged openly. The model is intentionally "compact and swift" — they're explicitly positioning it as a foundation layer, with the next generation already in development.

Here's the actually interesting part: Meta's framing has shifted. This isn't open-weight Llama anymore — Muse Spark is closed. The blog post is careful, the benchmarks are curated, and the access is controlled. Meta, which spent years positioning itself as the open alternative to the OpenAI monoculture, has quietly joined the closed-model club. Whether that's a strategic pivot or a temporary posture while they stabilize the new lab architecture doesn't matter much right now. The signal is: when serious money is on the table, open-weight becomes a liability.

Zuckerberg said he'll match whatever it costs to close the gap. Given that Anthropic just went from $9B to $30B ARR, he might be underestimating the tab.

## QUICK HITS
- **OpenAI, Anthropic, Google form distillation pact** — The three rivals are sharing intel through the Frontier Model Forum to detect and report Chinese adversarial distillation — using US model outputs to train competing models. Enemies when it's market share; friends when it's Beijing.
- **Anthropic may have closed the revenue gap on OpenAI** — Reuters reports Anthropic's ARR has reached $30B, up from $9B at end of 2025. OpenAI is still ahead but the gap is narrowing fast ahead of both companies' expected IPOs.
- **Poke brings AI agents to SMS** — No app, no API key, no setup: Poke routes agent workflows through text message. It's a clever wedge for non-developer users and a reminder that "agentic AI" doesn't have to mean a YAML config file and a Docker container.
- **Intel + SambaNova heterogeneous inference blueprint** — The two companies announced a joint architecture combining GPUs for prefill, SambaNova RDUs for decode, and Xeon 6 as host CPUs. Available H2 2026. The signal: GPU-only inference is becoming a bottleneck and the industry is starting to engineer around it.

## EM'S TAKE
Meta going closed is the story everyone's going to underreport because Muse Spark's benchmarks are just fine and fine doesn't write headlines. But the open-weight community built real infrastructure around Llama. Tools, fine-tunes, local deployments, entire businesses. Muse Spark doesn't feed that ecosystem. The developers who bet on Meta's open posture just got a quiet lesson in what "open" means when a lab decides it wants to compete seriously. Meanwhile the distillation pact is genuinely clever — three companies who hate each other agreeing to share information about a shared threat is the kind of alignment problem that actually gets solved when there's money and national security optics involved.
