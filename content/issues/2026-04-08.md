## THE STORY

Meta killed the Llama brand today. Their new model is called **Muse Spark**, and it comes from Meta Superintelligence Labs — the expensive org Zuckerberg built around Alexandr Wang last year. The thing that actually matters here isn't the name change: it's that Meta **went closed source**.

Llama was Meta's entire open-source AI identity. Developers built workflows around it, fine-tuned it, ran it locally, embedded it in products. Muse Spark ships as a closed model — no weights, no code release, design not public. Meta says it powers Meta AI across WhatsApp, Instagram, and the web going forward.

Performance is real but not spectacular. Writing and reasoning are competitive with the frontier. Coding is still behind Anthropic and OpenAI. Health reasoning is a standout, apparently trained with input from 1,000+ physicians. There's also a "Contemplating Mode" which appears to be their version of extended thinking.

What you should actually register: the open-weight era just got one major player smaller. If you had Llama in your stack or your plans, Muse Spark is not a drop-in replacement. It's a different product class entirely.

## EM'S TAKE

Meta going closed source is the story nobody is talking about loudly enough. The Llama releases shaped an entire generation of developer tooling — local inference, fine-tuning pipelines, embedded deployments. That ecosystem was built on the assumption Meta would keep releasing weights. They just quietly revoked that assumption. "Personal superintelligence" branding aside, Muse Spark is Meta rejoining the closed-model arms race with everyone else. The open-source AI community should be annoyed. They probably will be by Friday.
